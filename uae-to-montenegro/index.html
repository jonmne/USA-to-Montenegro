Common questions

Before you book

Will Montenegro residency trigger tax obligations in my home country?
This is the most common concern — and the most important to get right. Montenegro residency, structured correctly, does not automatically trigger tax residency in your home country. But the structure matters. That's exactly what we cover in the consultation: how to set it up so it works for your specific nationality and existing arrangements.
Do I have to physically live in Montenegro?
You need to be present for the initial permit application (a few days). For the annual residence permit, there is a minimum stay requirement only if you want to progress to permanent residency after five years. Many clients maintain their UAE base while holding Montenegro residency as a genuine second option — we'll cover what that looks like for your specific situation in the consultation.
How does this compare to Portugal, Malta or Cyprus programmes?
Montenegro is significantly faster (~90 days vs. 6–12 months), substantially cheaper (€2,100 for company formation vs. €250,000+ investment requirements), and doesn't require a large capital commitment. When Montenegro joins the EU, you'll hold EU residency — at a fraction of the cost of existing golden visa programmes.
Is Montenegro really joining the EU?
Montenegro is the most advanced candidate in the accession process. It opened negotiations in 2012 and has been closing chapters progressively. While no exact date is confirmed, it is widely expected within the next several years. The point is: residency obtained now will carry forward.

A full one-to-one session covering your specific situation — residency pathways, company formation, tax structuring, banking, real estate and cost of living. You'll receive a written summary afterwards with your recommended steps and document requirements. It's a working session, not a sales call.
Can I open a bank account in Montenegro?
Yes — both corporate and personal accounts. Banking setup is included in the full-service package once you proceed. We handle the introductions and documentation to make it straightforward. Multi-currency accounts are available at several banks.
I'm not ready to leave the UAE. Is this still relevant?
Absolutely — most of our UAE clients aren't leaving the Emirates. They're setting up a parallel base: residency, company, banking. A Plan B that's ready if and when they need it. The consultation will tell you exactly what that looks like for your situation.
